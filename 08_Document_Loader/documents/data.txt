Beneath a sky brushed soft with gold,
Where whispers of the morning fold,
The mountains breathe in silent grace,
A timeless calm, a sacred place.

A river hums its silver tune,
Reflecting light of rising noon,
While petals dance in gentle air,
As if the earth itself is prayer.

The wind moves slow through emerald trees,
Carrying songs upon the breeze,
Each leaf a verse, each branch a line,
In nature’s quiet grand design.

Beyond the hills, the sun dips low,
Painting the world in amber glow,
And in that hush, the heart can see—
The art of pure serenity.
